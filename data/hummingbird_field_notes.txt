Hummingbirds hover by beating their wings up to 80 times per second and can fly backward with remarkable control.
They favor tubular, nectar-rich flowers and remember reliable feeding spots with spatial precision.
Territorial displays include steep dives and rapid chirps; rivals yield when energy costs outweigh gains.

Migration can span thousands of kilometers; many species double body weight before crossing open water.
During torpor, metabolism drops dramatically to conserve energy through cold nights.
Banding studies show site fidelity, with individuals returning to the same gardens year over year.

Their hearts can exceed 1,200 beats per minute at peak exertion.
UV patterns on feathers guide mate selection, and bill curvature co-evolves with preferred blossoms.
Climate shifts are altering bloom timing, challenging the synchrony that sustains populations.
